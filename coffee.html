Roasted Coffee Beans

Roasting causes chemical changes to occur because the coffee beans are quickly brought to a very high temperature. When they reach the peak of perfection, they are quickly cooled to stop the process.


Roasted coffee beans have a different smell depending on the roast level, and they lose weight as the moisture has been removed. They are crunchy to the bite, ready to grind and brew.
